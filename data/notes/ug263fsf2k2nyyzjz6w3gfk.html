Curvilinear Differential Operators

Divergence and curl

For a vector field F\mathbf{F}F written in terms of curvilinear coordinates (x1,x2,x3)(x_1, x_2, x_3)(x1​,x2​,x3​)

F=F1e1+F2e2+F3e3,\mathbf{F} = F_1 \mathbf{e}_1 + F_2 \mathbf{e}_2 + F_3 \mathbf{e}_3,F=F1​e1​+F2​e2​+F3​e3​,

by definition of the scale factors and the orthogonality of the coordinate system. by definition of the partial derivative, and due to the fact that the value of F\mathbf{F}F remains roughly constant over an infinitesimal cuboid.

1h1h2h3[∂(F1h2h3)∂x1+∂(F2h1h3)∂x2+∂(F3h1h2)∂x3]\frac{1}{h_1h_2h_3}[\frac{\partial (F_1 h_2 h_3)}{\partial x_1} + \frac{\partial (F_2 h_1 h_3)}{\partial x_2} + \frac{\partial (F_3 h_1 h_2)}{\partial x_3}]h1​h2​h3​1​[∂x1​∂(F1​h2​h3​)​+∂x2​∂(F2​h1​h3​)​+∂x3​∂(F3​h1​h2​)​]

without the limit.

Correspondingly, we have the following result for curl:

Theorem. Curl in curvilinear coordinates for vector field in R3\mathbb{R^3}R3 denoted F\mathbf{F}F, curvilinear basis vectors e1,e2,e3\mathbf{e_1, e_2, e_3}e1​,e2​,e3​, and scale factors h1,h2,h3h_1, h_2, h_3h1​,h2​,h3​ is given by the determinant

∇×F=1h1h2h3∣h1e1h2e2h3e3∂1∂2∂3h1F1h2F2h3F3∣\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \frac{1}{h_1 h_2 h_3}\begin{vmatrix} h_1 \mathbf{e_1} & h_2 \mathbf{e_2} & h_3 \mathbf{e_3} \\ \partial_1 & \partial_2 & \partial_3 \\ h_1F_1 & h_2F_2 & h_3 F_3 \end{vmatrix}∇×F=h1​h2​h3​1​∣∣​h1​e1​∂1​h1​F1​​h2​e2​∂2​h2​F2​​h3​e3​∂3​h3​F3​​∣∣​

(This differs from the standard expression for curl, given in Cartesian orthonormal basis vectors as

∣exeyek∂x∂y∂zFxFyFz∣\begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{e_x} & \mathbf{e_y} & \mathbf{e_k} \\ \partial_x & \partial_y & \partial_z \\ F_x & F_y & F_z \end{vmatrix}∣∣​ex​∂x​Fx​​ey​∂y​Fy​​ek​∂z​Fz​​∣∣​

by only the presence of scale factors.)

Proof.

Begin as above with the integral definition of curl, given by

n⋅(∇×F)=lim⁡A→0∮CF⋅dxA\mathbf{n} \cdot (\nabla \times \mathbf{F}) = \lim_{A \to 0}\frac{\oint_C \mathbf{F \cdot dx}}{A}n⋅(∇×F)=A→0lim​A∮C​F⋅dx​

for an infinitesimally small curve CCC enclosing an area AAA, and n\mathbf{n}n as the normal vector to that area. Suppose we choose the normal vector e1\mathbf{e_1}e1​; this would give us the first component of the vector representing curl in the basis e1,e2,e3\mathbf{e_1, e_2, e_3}e1​,e2​,e3​.

Consider an infinitesimally small (rectangular) area on a surface normal to e1\mathbf{e_1}e1​; in other words, the area has tangent vectors e2\mathbf{e_2}e2​ and e3\mathbf{e_3}e3​, as they are themselves orthonormal to e1\mathbf{e_1}e1​. As such, the area can be approximated by

δx2δx3(h2h3)\delta x_2 \delta x_3 (h_2h_3)δx2​δx3​(h2​h3​)

by definition of the scale factors; the total circulation of F\mathbf{F}F over the curve which bounds that area is thus the vector-valued line integral over a rectangle whose sides are parallel to e2\mathbf{e_2}e2​ and e3\mathbf{e_3}e3​. For instance, for the pair of sides parallel to e2\mathbf{e_2}e2​ and running in opposite directions, we have

e2⋅(F(x1,x2,x3+δx3)−F(x1,x2,x3))(length of line)\mathbf{e_2} \cdot (\mathbf{F}(x_1,x_2, x_3+\delta x_3) - \mathbf{F}(x_1,x_2,x_3)) (\text{length of line})e2​⋅(F(x1​,x2​,x3​+δx3​)−F(x1​,x2​,x3​))(length of line)

equalling

(F2(x1,x2,x3x3)−F2(x1,x2,x3+δ))(δx2h2)=−∂(F2h2)∂x3δx2δx3(\mathbf{F_2}(x_1,x_2, x_3 x_3) - \mathbf{F_2}(x_1,x_2,x_3+\delta)) (\delta x_2 h_2) = -\frac{\partial (F_2 h_2)}{\partial x_3}\delta x_2 \delta x_3(F2​(x1​,x2​,x3​x3​)−F2​(x1​,x2​,x3​+δ))(δx2​h2​)=−∂x3​∂(F2​h2​)​δx2​δx3​

as e3\mathbf{e_3}e3​ is "going downwards" in a right-handed basis, and correspondingly, for the pair of sides parallel to e3\mathbf{e_3}e3​, we have

∂(F3h3)∂x2δx2δx3\frac{\partial(F_3 h_3)}{\partial x_2}\delta x_2 \delta x_3∂x2​∂(F3​h3​)​δx2​δx3​

positive because e2\mathbf{e_2}e2​ is "going upwards" in a right-handed basis. This gives us

e1⋅(∇×F)=lim⁡δx2,δx3→0(∂2(F3h3)−∂3(F2h2))(δx2δx3)(h2h3)(δx2δx3)=1h2h3∣∂2∂3F2h2F3h3∣\mathbf{e_1}\cdot (\nabla \times \mathbf{F}) = \lim_{\delta x_2, \delta x_3 \to 0}\frac{(\partial_2(F_3 h_3) - \partial_3(F_2 h_2))(\delta x_2\delta x_3)}{(h_2h_3)(\delta x_2 \delta x_3)} = \frac{1}{h_2 h_3}\begin{vmatrix} \partial_2 & \partial_3 \\ F_2 h_2 & F_3 h_3 \end{vmatrix}e1​⋅(∇×F)=δx2​,δx3​→0lim​(h2​h3​)(δx2​δx3​)(∂2​(F3​h3​)−∂3​(F2​h2​))(δx2​δx3​)​=h2​h3​1​∣∣​∂2​F2​h2​​∂3​F3​h3​​∣∣​

as the first term for our expression for curl. The other two terms follow suit in a similar manner, with choices of e2\mathbf{e_2}e2​ and e3\mathbf{e_3}e3​ as their normal vectors. In total, we have

∇×F=1h1h2h3∣h1e1h2e2h3e3∂1∂2∂3h1F1h2F2h3F3∣\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \frac{1}{h_1 h_2 h_3}\begin{vmatrix} h_1 \mathbf{e_1} & h_2\mathbf{e_2} & h_3\mathbf{e_3} \\ \partial_1 & \partial_2 & \partial_3 \\ h_1 F_1 & h_2 F_2 & h_3 F_3 \end{vmatrix}∇×F=h1​h2​h3​1​∣∣​h1​e1​∂1​h1​F1​​h2​e2​∂2​h2​F2​​h3​e3​∂3​h3​F3​​∣∣​
