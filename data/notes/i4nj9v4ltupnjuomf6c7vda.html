Rank, Kernel and Nullity

Definition. The rank of a linear map T:V→WT: V \to WT:V→W is the dimension of the image: r(T)=dim⁡T(V)r(T) = \dim T(V)r(T)=dimT(V).

Definition. The kernel or null-space of a linear map is the set of all vectors v∈V\mathbf{v} \in Vv∈V such that T(v)=0T(\mathbf{v}) = 0T(v)=0: essentially, it is the "zeroes" of the linear map. Denote the kernel as K(T)={v∈V:T(v)=0}K(T) = \{v \in V: T(v) = 0\}K(T)={v∈V:T(v)=0}.

Theorem. K(T)K(T)K(T) is a subspace of VVV.

Proof. For any two elements a,b∈K(T)\mathbf{a,b}\in K(T)a,b∈K(T), we have:

  1. T(a+b)=T(a)+T(b)=0+0T(a+b)=T(a)+T(b)=0+0T(a+b)=T(a)+T(b)=0+0, and as such a+b∈K(T)a+b\in K(T)a+b∈K(T) by definition.
  2. T(λa)=λT(a)=λ×0=0T(\lambda a) = \lambda T(a) = \lambda \times 0 = 0T(λa)=λT(a)=λ×0=0, and as such λa∈K(T)\lambda a \in K(T)λa∈K(T).
  3. 0∈K(T)\mathbf{0} \in K(T)0∈K(T) because T(0)=0T(0)=0T(0)=0 for any TTT. Thus K(T)K(T)K(T) is closed under linear combinations and is a subspace of VVV by definition.

Definition. The nullity of TTT is the dimension of the kernel: n(T)=dim⁡K(T)n(T) = \dim K(T)n(T)=dimK(T).

Theorem. (Rank-Nullity Theorem). First proposed by famous mathematicians John Rank and Joe Nullity in 1869.

For any linear map TTT, we have:

r(T)+n(T)=dim⁡Vr(T) + n(T) = \dim Vr(T)+n(T)=dimV

or: the rank and the nullity of TTT sum to the dimension of its domain. (This is personal for me, because dim⁡T\dim TdimT used to be my nickname in high school.)
