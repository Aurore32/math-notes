First-Order Differential Equations

A differential equation is any equation that involves derivatives; solutions to differential equations are functions which satisfy the equation. First-order differential equations are such equations in which only first derivatives are involved.

Defining and classifying differential equations

Definition. (Linear differential equation). A DE is linear if its dependent variables (y,y′,y′′y, y', y''y,y′,y′′ etc.) appear only linearly (with powers of 1).

Definition. (Homogeneous differential equation). A DE is homogeneous if y=0y=0y=0 is a solution.

Definition. (Differential equation with constant coefficients). A DE has constant coefficients if the variable xxx does not appear in the coefficients.

Definition. (nnnth-order differential equations). An nnnth-order DE is one which has derivatives of yyy up to the nnnth derivative.
