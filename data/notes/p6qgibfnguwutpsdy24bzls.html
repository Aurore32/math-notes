Roots of Unity

Definition (Roots of unity). We refer to the complex roots of the equation ωn=1\omega^n=1ωn=1 as the nth roots of unity; as this is a polynomial of deg nnn, we have nnn roots of unity, which can be completely described by

ω=e2πkin, k=0,1,2,3,...,(n−1).\omega=e^{\frac{2\pi ki}{n}},\ k=0,1,2,3,...,(n-1).ω=en2πki​, k=0,1,2,3,...,(n−1).
