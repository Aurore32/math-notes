Difference Equations

Difference equations are the discrete counterpart to differential equations; in essence, whereas differential equations are concerned with the rate of change of a continuous function, difference equations concern the change of a sequence. For instance, in the logistic population model, instead of a continuous change in population, population may change per season (spring or winter).

Definition. We define a difference equation using terms similar to that of differential equations. For difference equation in y(x)y(x)y(x), if yn−1y_{n-1}yn−1​ is analogous to y(x)y(x)y(x), then yny_{n}yn​ is analogous to y′y'y′, yn+1y_{n+1}yn+1​ to y′′y''y′′, etc. We can also classify difference equations in similar ways to differential equations:

  1. Whether the equation is linear, i.e. whether it features powers of yyy beyond the first.
  2. Whether the equation is homogeneous, i.e. features the variable xxx.
  3. The order of the equation, i.e. how many of yn,yn−1,...y_{n}, y_{n-1}, ...yn​,yn−1​,... does it feature?
