Subspaces

Definition (Vector subspace). We say that UUU is a subspace of a vector space VVV if U⊆VU \subseteq VU⊆V and UUU is also a vector space under the same operations (addition and scalar multiplication).

Of the subspaces of VVV, VVV and {0}\{0\}{0} are trivially subspaces; the other subspaces are proper subspaces.

If UUU is a subspace of VVV, then by the definition of a vector space, UUU must also be closed under addition and scalar multiplication: that is, for a,b∈U\mathbf{a,b}\in Ua,b∈U and some scalar λ\lambdaλ, we have

  1. a+b∈U\mathbf{a+b}\in Ua+b∈U
  2. λa∈U\lambda\mathbf{a} \in Uλa∈U
  3. 0∈U\mathbf{0} \in U0∈U.

    In other words, UUU is closed under linear transformation λa+μb\lambda\mathbf{a}+\mu\mathbf{b}λa+μb.
