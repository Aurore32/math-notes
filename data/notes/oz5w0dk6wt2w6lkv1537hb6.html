Second-Order Differential Equations

Definition. A second-order ordinary differential equation in xxx and yyy is one whose highest-order derivative of yyy is the second derivative. Most methods that apply to second-order ODEs also apply to higher-order equations as well.

Definition. A linear second-order ODE is one that does not have any higher powers of y,y′,y′′y, y', y''y,y′,y′′ beyond the 1st power. As non-linear second-order ODEs are difficult to discuss analytically, the following sections will focus on solutions to linear ODEs.

Definition. Homogeneous second-order ODEs are ones that can be written in the form P(x)y′′+Q(x)y′+R(x)=0P(x)y'' + Q(x)y' + R(x) = 0P(x)y′′+Q(x)y′+R(x)=0 (RHS is 0); inhomogeneous equations are such that the RHS is non-zero (some function of xxx).

As discussed, once the corresponding homogeneous equation has been solved, the inhomogeneous equation soon follows; the more fundamental problem is the solution of the inhomogeneous equation.
