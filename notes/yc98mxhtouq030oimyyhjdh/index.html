What are group actions?

At the very beginning of this course, we were instructed to de-abstractify groups by thinking of them as something that rotates a Rubik's Cube. (Rubik's Cubes were only invented in the 1970s, which means people in the 1960s probably learned group theory using disco balls.) Then we were taught the virtues of groups as abstract objects, where we shoved about 710e697^{10^{e^{69}}}710e69 different definitions of amusing words like "homomorphisms" down our throats and refused to acknowledge the existence of real-world things like triangles or cubes.
