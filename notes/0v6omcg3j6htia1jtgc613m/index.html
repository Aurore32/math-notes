Canonical Forms

Definition. A canonical form for a 2×22\times 22×2 matrix includes the following:

  1. an enlargement along the axes [λ100λ2]\begin{bmatrix} \lambda_1 & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda_2 \end{bmatrix}[λ1​0​0λ2​​],
  2. a shear [λ10λ]\begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 1 \\ 0 & \lambda \end{bmatrix}[λ0​1λ​], and
  3. scalar multiples of the identity matrix [λ00λ]\begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda \end{bmatrix}[λ0​0λ​].

Theorem. All 2×22\times 22×2 matrices are similar to one of the above canonical forms.

Proof.

Which canonical form the matrix is similar to depends on its eigenvalues: whether it has two identical eigenvalues, or two distinct eigenvalues.
