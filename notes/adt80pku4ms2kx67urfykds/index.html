Higher-order partial derivatives

Definition. Higher-order partial derivatives are partial derivatives of lower-order partial derivatives. For instance, a second-order partial derivative for a function f(x,y)f(x, y)f(x,y) could be

Theorem. The mixed second-order partial derivatives of f(x,y)f(x,y)f(x,y), fxyf_{xy}fxy​ and fyxf_{yx}fyx​, are equal at a point (x0,y0)(x_0, y_0)(x0​,y0​) if and only if at least one of fxyf_{xy}fxy​ and fyxf_{yx}fyx​ are continuous at (x0,y0)(x_0, y_0)(x0​,y0​).

Proof. Trivial.

This is a consequence of the Divergence Theorem.
