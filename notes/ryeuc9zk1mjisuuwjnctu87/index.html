Quotient Groups

The normal vector to a plane is a vector which has exactly nothing in common with any vector on the plane. The normal distribution is a distribution that somehow manages to pull eee and π\piπ out of its ass even without being named after Euler. The normal subgroup is a subgroup that satisfies the extremely intuitive and not-at-all whack-ass condition of gHg−1=HgHg^{-1} = HgHg−1=H. I'm starting to feel like mathematicians actually have no idea what the word "normal" means.
